Dr. B. R. Ambedkar National Institute of Technology Jalandhar is a public engineering institute of national importance under the Ministry of Education, Government of India. It was established in 1987 as Regional Engineering College Jalandhar and was declared an NIT in 2002. The institute is located on GT Road near Amritsar Bypass in Jalandhar, Punjab, and has an urban campus spread over approximately 154 acres.

NIT Jalandhar offers undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs across engineering, science, and management disciplines. Undergraduate programs include B.Tech degrees in Computer Science and Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Civil Engineering, Electronics and Communication Engineering, Chemical Engineering, Biotechnology, and related fields.

Postgraduate programs at NIT Jalandhar include M.Tech, M.Sc, and MBA degrees. The institute also offers PhD programs across various academic departments and interdisciplinary centers. Admissions to postgraduate programs follow national-level examinations and centralized counseling processes.

Admissions to B.Tech programs at NIT Jalandhar are conducted through JEE Main followed by JoSAA and CSAB counseling. Seat allocation is based on rank, category, and availability as published in the official JoSAA seat matrix. The institute follows reservation policies as per Government of India norms.

M.Tech admissions at NIT Jalandhar are based on GATE scores and conducted through CCMT counseling. MBA admissions consider national-level entrance examinations such as CAT, MAT, or CMAT, followed by further selection processes if applicable. PhD admissions are conducted through institute-level entrance tests and interviews, with exemptions for candidates holding valid national fellowships.

NIT Jalandhar follows a credit-based semester system for all academic programs. Academic calendars, examination schedules, syllabi, and regulations are published on the institute website each semester. Programs include theory courses, laboratory courses, projects, internships, and thesis components depending on the level of study.

The institute has multiple academic departments including Computer Science and Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Civil Engineering, Electronics and Communication Engineering, Chemical Engineering, and Biotechnology. Each department maintains faculty profiles, research areas, laboratory facilities, and course syllabi on their respective webpages.

The Training and Placement Cell at NIT Jalandhar facilitates internships and final placements for students. In recent placement cycles, the institute has reported a placement rate of approximately 91.88 percent. The highest package offered has reached up to 52 LPA, with an average package of around 9.78 LPA.

NIT Jalandhar placement processes typically include pre-placement talks, aptitude or technical tests, technical interviews, and HR interviews. Major recruiters include leading multinational companies and Indian organizations across IT, core engineering, analytics, and research sectors.

NIT Jalandhar provides separate hostel facilities for boys and girls within the campus. Hostel allocation is based on the program and year of study. Hostels are equipped with basic furniture, Wi-Fi connectivity, mess facilities, power backup, and security arrangements.

The campus of NIT Jalandhar is fully residential and includes academic blocks, hostels, administrative buildings, sports facilities, and green spaces. The institute offers facilities for indoor and outdoor sports, a gymnasium, medical center, library, and internet connectivity.

Student life at NIT Jalandhar includes participation in technical, cultural, literary, and social clubs. The institute organizes an annual technical festival named TechNITi along with various cultural events, workshops, seminars, and guest lectures throughout the academic year.

NIT Jalandhar has a structured faculty and administrative system. Faculty members are responsible for teaching, research, curriculum development, and student mentoring. The institute is governed by a Board of Governors, with the Director serving as the chief academic and executive head.
